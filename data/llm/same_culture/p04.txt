Gulistan chapter II - story 5

Several travelers were on a journey together, sharing each other's hardships and comforts equally. I wished to join them, but they refused. I said, "It is not the way of noble men to turn away from the company of the poor and lose the chance to benefit from them. I consider myself strong and able to help, not a burden. Even if I do not ride an animal, I can assist in carrying the blankets." One of them replied, "Do not be upset by what you hear. Some days ago, a thief disguised as a dervish came and joined us."

How can people know a person’s true nature by their clothes? The writer knows what is truly inside the book. Since dervishes are known for their humble appearance, the others had no suspicion and welcomed the thief as a friend. The outward appearance of the seekers of truth is their patched clothing, which is enough to show who they are to others. But strive to be good in your deeds, no matter what you wear. Put a crown on your head or a flag on your back if you wish; true holiness lies in abandoning the world, lust, and desire—not just in wearing a robe. A man must show courage and strength in battle. What good are weapons to someone who lacks true manhood?

We traveled until nightfall and camped near a fort. The shameless thief took a companion’s water pot, pretending he was going to perform ablutions, but he left to steal. A fake saint who wears the dervish’s garb has turned the sacred robes of the Ka'bah into a cover for a donkey. After slipping away unseen by the dervishes, he reached a tower and stole a chest. By dawn, the evil man had fled far away. When the others awoke, innocent and unaware, they were all arrested and thrown into prison at the fort. From then on, we gave up traveling in company and chose the path of solitude, following the saying: Safety lies in being alone.

When one from a group does wrong, no honor remains for anyone, whether low or high. Don’t you see how one ox in the pasture can stain all the oxen in the village? I replied, "Thanks be to God, I have not been denied the benefits that dervishes enjoy, even though I have left their company. What you have told me is valuable, and this warning will serve those like me throughout life."

For one rude man in a gathering, the hearts of wise men are deeply saddened. If a tank is filled with rose water, a dog falling into it will pollute the whole.

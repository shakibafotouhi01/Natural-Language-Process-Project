The faith of a starving man

Once, there was a wandering dervish known for his deep faith and devotion. He was lost in the desert, starving and desperate for food. As he walked along an old path, he spotted an empty fruit sack left by someone who had passed before. Gratefully, he picked it up, slung it over his shoulder, and prayed aloud, “Thank God for giving a starving man an empty fruit sack,” before continuing on.

A little farther ahead, he found an old hunting bow on the ground, but its string was broken. He placed the bow into his sack and again gave thanks aloud, “Thank God for giving a starving man a hunting bow with a broken string.”

Further along the road, he saw a dead tree with no fruit. He broke off some dry branches and added them to his sack, once more praying aloud, “Thank God for guiding a starving man to a dead fruit tree.”

As he walked on, he discovered a battered cooking pot lying on the ground. After blowing off the dust, he put it into his sack and thanked God aloud again, “Thank God for giving a starving man a dented old pot full of dust.”

Later, he found a lone fishing hook but no pole to go with it. He picked up the hook, dropped it into his sack, and reiterated his gratitude loudly, “Thank God for giving a starving man a fishing hook without a fishing pole.”

After many days of wandering, the path finally brought him to a vast river, so wide he could not see the opposite shore. The old dervish sank to his knees by the water’s edge and prayed loudly, “Thank God for leading a starving man to a river so wide that crossing it seems impossible.”

Then, with his calm spirit and faith, he tied the fishing hook to the broken bowstring, using the bow as a makeshift fishing rod. Using the dry branches, he built a fire and cooked the fish he caught in the old pot, finally nourishing himself with a meal born of patience and trust.

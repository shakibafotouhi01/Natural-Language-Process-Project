Raven Steals the Sun

In the time before memory, when the dense German forests were shrouded in endless night, the world lay in total darkness.

The Rabe, a cunning black bird who had existed since the beginning of days, grew weary of stumbling blindly through the dark woods and mist. 

One evening, the Rabe discovered the small cottage of a solitary old man who lived deep in the Schwarzwald with his young daughter. Through quiet observation and craft, the Rabe learned that the old man possessed a priceless treasure: all the light in the world, locked away inside a tiny wooden box, nested within box after box, hidden beneath thick woolen cloths embroidered with ancient runes.

Determined, the Rabe vowed to bring light back to the shadowed forests.

He pondered long and devised a clever plan. When the old man’s daughter came to draw water from the cold, dark river, the Rabe transformed himself into a single, sharp spruce needle and drifted gently down the current, slipping into her wooden bucket.

As she drank, the needle slid down her throat and settled deep within her warmth. There, the Rabe changed once more, becoming a small human child. Time passed slowly in the dim silence of her womb, until finally the Rabe was born into the world as a peculiar boy.

Though marked by his strange looks, the old man’s heart softened, and he loved this child as a grandson. Yet he fiercely warned that no one should ever touch the sacred box of light. Despite this, the Rabe child begged to hold the glowing treasure even for a moment.

At last, the old man relented. He lifted the small box’s lid and revealed a radiant, glowing orb that pulsed like a tiny sun. He threw it gently toward his grandson.

As the glowing orb approached, the boy transformed rapidly into a great black bird, its vast wings unfurling like storm clouds over the forest canopy, its sharp beak wide and ready. With a triumphant croak, the Rabe caught the orb in his beak.

With mighty flaps, he burst through the cottage’s thatched roof, flying out through the smoke hole into the night sky, his prize shining like a beacon in his beak.

Thus, from that day forward, the light returned to brighten the forests and fields, chasing away the long shadows, and filling the world with day once more.

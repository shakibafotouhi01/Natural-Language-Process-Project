Gulistan chapter II - story 5

Long ago, a band of travelers journeyed together across the vast plains and dense forests, sharing their burdens and blessings alike. I wished to join their circle, to walk alongside them and share their path, but they would not permit it. I spoke to them earnestly: "Among proud warriors and wise elders, it is not the way to turn a face from those who walk humbly, nor to deny oneself the strength found in such company. Though I lack a horse, my spirit is strong—I can carry our blankets and ease your load." Yet one among them replied, "Do not be offended by our words. Not long ago, a false brother, cloaked like a medicine man, came and joined us, but his heart was dark."

How can one truly know the spirit beneath a painted face? The storyteller knows the truth the tale must hold.

Because the medicine men are trusted, they welcomed him with open hands and hearts, believing him a friend.

The true wise men wear humble garments of patched leather and soft cloth, showing only their honest selves to others.

Let deeds be your adornment, no matter the robe you wear. Place feathers in your hair and carry your staff with pride. To walk the sacred path means to empty one’s heart of greed and desire—not merely to cast aside outer garments. True strength shows itself in the battles of life. What good are weapons to one who cannot face the fight with courage?

One night, as we traveled under the quiet gaze of the moon, we made camp near a watchful stone fort. The false brother, lifting a water gourd from a companion as though to cleanse his hands in ritual, slipped away into the shadows to steal.

A pretender who wears the healer’s mantle  
Turns the sacred eagle feathers into mere tassels for an enemy’s horse.

He vanished from our sight and climbed a tall watchtower, where he took a box of precious things. By dawn, the black-hearted stranger had gone far along the trail. When morning came, the innocent, still asleep in camp, were seized and cast into the cold shadow of the fort. From that day forward, we turned from fellowship to solitude, living by this wisdom: safety lies in walking alone.

When one soul in a tribe acts in dishonor,  
The shame drapes over both the great and the small.  
Have you not seen how one wild horse  
Taints the spirit of the entire herd?

I answered them with gratitude: “Great is the Spirit of the Earth and Sky! Though I walk apart from the medicine men, I have not been barred from their blessings. Your story has reached my heart and will guide me through all my days.”

For one brash wolf among the pack,  
The hearts of wise elders carry a heavy sorrow.  
If a river runs sweet with clear water,  
A wolf diving in can muddy it all.

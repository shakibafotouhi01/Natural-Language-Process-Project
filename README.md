Stories We Tell (and the Machines Retell)
Project Description

This repository contains the final project for the Natural Language Processing course taught by Prof. Alfio Ferrara.
The project investigates how Large Language Models (LLMs) represent, reproduce, and transform narrative archetypes across different cultural traditions.

The central goal is to analyze whether common storytelling structures—such as conflict–resolution arcs, transformation narratives, or archetypal roles—emerge as universal cognitive patterns or whether they are shaped by cultural context. To this end, the project compares human-written narratives with LLM-generated retellings, aiming to uncover both shared deep narrative structures and cultural biases in automated storytelling.

Narratives are modeled as structured representations (event graphs or narrative networks), enabling comparison through graph-based and semantic similarity measures. LLMs are prompted to retell stories across cultural contexts, allowing us to evaluate how narrative logic is preserved, altered, or hybridized during generation.
Beyond analysis, the project also explores the idea of LLMs as potential “cultural translators”, capable of adapting myths and stories across symbolic and cultural systems.
